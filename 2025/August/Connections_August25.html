Listening, learning, & inspiring healthy communities

Good day,

All throughout this spring and summer, we've been on the road traveling to communities across the state to meet with providers and organizations that serve Merdian’s members.

In our series of provider town halls, we are engaging proactively in open dialogue with our provider partners to let them know we are listening and we care. The insights they’ve shared have been essential to improving outcomes for our members and strengthening trust. Our provider partners’ real‑world experiences and observations inform our approach, enhance our delivery of services, and ensure we remain aligned in our shared goal to help Meridian’s members live their healthiest lives.

I’m proud to share that we’re uniquely local, with staff living in communities we serve all over Illinois. This is a key strength in cultivating partnerships to help us better understand the needs of the greater community, as well as those of our members. You’ll see this up close in Lucas’s story in the Meridian member voices section. His story is a good example of how listening deeply and thoughtful solutioning can make a lasting impact.

As we continue our travels across the state, you’ll see us sharing health and nutrition information at countless community events, marching in parades, handing out school supplies, and providing onsite screening services. In Springfield, we’re supporting the expansion of “From Seeds to Tables,” a program that teaches youth important life skills while fostering a direct connection between nature and nutrition (See the Care in the Community section below). We are also partnering with the Chicago Fire to bring health education and youth programs to more than 25 neighborhoods in Chicago all summer long. These opportunities to listen, learn, and connect are truly invaluable.

Our presence in the community reflects our commitment to meaningful engagement. Whether it’s in a provider town hall or at a back‑to‑school event, you’ll find Meridian in places where connection and impact matter.

Best wishes,

Cristal Gary

Meridian Plan President and CEO

Supporting our YouthCare members & families

Foster child quickly receives much-needed care from specialist

Cody recently entered care with the Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS). He has a growth hormone deficiency and is followed by endocrinologists. DCFS had limited information about Cody’s medical needs and notified YouthCare of the situation. Our GuidedCare team assigned Cody a registered nurse care manager. Two business days later, Cody saw an endocrine specialist for his condition. Throughout his care, we continue to work closely with DCFS to support Cody’s overall well‑being.

Care in our Community

Springfield youth dig into gardening program

A Meridian Health Happens Here Grant™ of $40,000 has helped Motherland Gardens grow its 6‑week summer program, “From Seeds to Table,” into a robust, year‑round effort. The expansion of the program allows Springfield area students in grades K through 12 to engage in continuous hands-on learning in gardening, nutrition, and the culinary arts.

Throughout the year, participating youth plant and maintain gardens, learn seasonal growing techniques, and harvest the produce they help nurture. They also gain practical cooking skills by preparing healthy meals with the food they grow, fostering a direct connection between agriculture and nutrition.

More than just a gardening program, “From Seeds to Table” collaborates with local schools and community organizations to empower underserved youth by addressing food security and providing structured opportunities to develop vital life and workforce skills like leadership development and conflict resolution. The year-round format deepens its impact‑supporting academic engagement, boosting self-esteem, and building resilience. As youth grow their knowledge and confidence, they also grow in their role as contributors and changemakers within their communities.
